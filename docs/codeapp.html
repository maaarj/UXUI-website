Project timeline

4 months (August 2021 - December 2021)

#Code app

#Code app is a learning application for beginners in learning #C. We aim to keep users engaged and make students feel fulfilled through bite-size lessons, motivating copy, study streak, downloadable content and certificate.

#Code app is a course requirement for our Mobile development class. The requirement is for us to develop any Android application and we chose to work on what is common for us which is learning programming languages.

Users

The application is for Computer science and IT students or anyone who is just starting to learn programming languages.

The problem

Learning programming languages can be daunting and exhausting to learn because of the technicalities. For the 3 students I interviewed, this resulted to procrastination, distress and feeling demotivated in general.

How might we minimize the negative emotion in studying programming languages?

Flowchart

I shared a flowchart with the team to serve as our common language and easily map out the flow. For the designers, we used this to make sure there are no dead-ends on the design.

Wireframes

I worked with one UI designer for the wireframes and we iterated between wireframing and getting feedback from other teammates until we are aligned with the objectives.

Color palette & Typography

We involed the team in suggesting and picking the color and typography of the app. We made 3 different high-fidelity wireframes with their suggestions. These were presented with the members to help us visualize and get the feel of each color and typography when applied on the wireframes.

Lawn Green & Poppins

In the end we decided to go with Lawn green because of it's energetic feel with the light theme.

UI Design

I went through two iterations for the prototype before I began to develop the UI. The prototype is then presented and approved by our Professor which acts as our client. Our app is in light theme with light shade of green inspired by Duolingo app.

Next steps

If future plans include publishing the application in Google Playstore, I would highly recommend to test it with users first and iterate from there.

Technology is a fast-changing industry, same as the needs of each student. C# is one of the foundational programming languages one could learn and it's important to keep their needs in check to update not only the features but also the content.

What I learned from this project

Research can never fail you. No matter how small the research effort is, one way or the other it can always teach you something.

One of the most common mistake I wanted to avoid in my career is just checking the list of deliverables without a solid reason. Here I was able to get a sense of which deliverables serves the team the best.

I'm most thankful for my team for the meaningful discussions I had with them throughout the development process. Their support made me appreciate more of the field I'm pursuing right now.
