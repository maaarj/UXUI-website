Trash Panda

Due to the increase of plastic waste during the pandemic, people who are conscious about their waste looked for another way to dispose their recyclables. Trash panda offers a pick-up service for their recyclables and assurance that it will go to proper facilities.

To further understand their unique personality, needs and pain points, I reached out to people from their Facebook posts and invited them for interview or usability testing. This led me for a partial redesign to easily find information, have a systematic way to cancel booking, and system feedback.

The process

My first step in research is by reading through comments on their Facebook page to see the bigger picture first and used this opportunity to reach out to invite them for interview or usability testing.

After analyzing and collecting relevant data from Facebook page, interview and one round of usability testing for the current design, I grouped them into themes to easily recognize and prioritize the problems.

I prioritized problems based on how frequent the theme occurs in the research phase.

Insights from comments, interviews and usability testing

  • When they are not sure about the classifications, they ask through commenting on a Facebook post.
  • Some get an impression that the service is free since it’s garbage collection or they are partnered with their local government.
  • They want to know how much the base rate is to compare it against other services or drop-off points.
  • Users who already booked, they usually check their emails or wait for an open slot and simulate a booking to calculate the fees.
  • They are expecting to see a map with the driver’s location to know when to expect them.
  • Users assume they have to reach out through Facebook messenger to cancel their booking.

Objectives for the redesign

  • Improve information findability to provide users key information about the service.
  • Maintain straightforward and simplistic approach of the application
  • Have a systematic approach in tracking booking cancellations from users.

Reflections and lessons learnt

#1 - The taste of the real experience

I started very nervous but very excited about this project because I treated this as a real one. After the process, I feel very fulfilled and excited to think about the next case study. This helped me to realize that I

  • enjoy generating ideas or solutions
  • sketching those solutions
  • keeping the users and the business aligned
  • it's okay to fail as long as you learn from it.

From that, I was able to drop the things I overthink about and do the best I could.
