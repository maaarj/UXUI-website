Project timeline

Trash Panda

Due to the increase of plastic waste during the pandemic, people who are conscious about their waste looked for another way to dispose their recyclables. Trash panda offers a pick-up service for their recyclables and assurance that it will go to proper facilities.

To further understand their unique personality, needs and pain points, I reached out to people from their Facebook posts and invited them for interview or usability testing. This led me for a partial redesign to easily find information, have a systematic way to cancel booking, and system feedback.

Disclaimer: I'm not affiliated with Trash Panda

Why the redesign?

Although users find the app pretty straight-forward and easy to use because of it’s simplicity, they still tend to look for information through their Facebook page, website, or their email when they can’t access those information through the app.

Prior doing user research, I discovered that there is no means for the user to cancel their booking in the app. Users will, instead, cancel their booking by messaging their Facebook page. For some instances, this can also have an impact to Trash panda’s resources since fees are not paid upfront upon booking.

The process

My first step in research is by reading through comments on their Facebook page to see the bigger picture first and used this opportunity to reach out to invite them for interview or usability testing.

After analyzing and collecting relevant data from Facebook page, interview and one round of usability testing for the current design, I grouped them into themes to easily recognize and prioritize the problems.

I prioritized problems based on how frequent the theme occurs in the research phase.

Insights from comments, interviews and usability testing

  • When they are not sure about the classifications, they ask through commenting on a Facebook post.
  • Some get an impression that the service is free since it’s garbage collection or they are partnered with their local government.
  • They want to know how much the base rate is to compare it against other services or drop-off points.
  • Users who already booked, they usually check their emails or wait for an open slot and simulate a booking to calculate the fees.
  • They are expecting to see a map with the driver’s location to know when to expect them.
  • Users assume they have to reach out through Facebook messenger to cancel their booking.

Objectives for the redesign

  • Improve information findability to provide users key information about the service.
  • Maintain straightforward and simplistic approach of the application
  • Have a systematic approach in tracking booking cancellations from users.

Sketches

I sketched out a few possible solutions from the ideation stage. This helped me to focus on the layout rather than aesthetics or design details and allowed me to freely generate as many ideas as I can.

Redesigning

#1 - Information findability

I converted the kebab menu into hamburger menu and placed secondary information that users need. This way the secondary information have dedicated screens for them and the homescreen is focused on their impact and schedule details.

Bottom nav bar - to let users feel in control by freely navigate through the app and access information like fee calculator and accepted recyclables.

I removed the ‘Book now’ button and replace them with open dates and their booked date with a booking status below.

#2 - Cancel booking

Cancel button - I added this on the Booking summary for ease of access, and added a note when the button will be disabled. This can also be easily seen by Trash Panda.

#3 - System status & Feedback

Adding confirmation popups and second confirmation to let the users know that the system received their requests or option to undo request.

#4 - Progress trackers

I replaced the step numbers on the top left part of the booking process with a progress tracker or wizard to help the users know how many steps they have to complete, where they are in the process, and which steps they have completed.

Testing

To validate my changes to the current design, I asked the usability participants to do these tasks again using Maze and what they have to say.

Reflections and lessons learnt

#1 - The taste of the real experience

I started very nervous but very excited about this project because I treated this as a real one. After the process, I feel very fulfilled and excited to think about the next case study. This helped me to realize that I

  • enjoy generating ideas or solutions
  • sketching those solutions
  • keeping the users and the business aligned
  • it's okay to fail as long as you learn from it.

From that, I was able to drop the things I overthink about and do the best I could.

#2 Recruitment and user research

It was a good experience to talk and reach out to total strangers for the interview, it gave me an idea of how a real user interview will be and prepared me for the next case study.

The recruitment was more of a challenge than the interview since I relied on Facebook page rather than joining groups that have the same goal as Trash Panda, at the same time.

#3 - Validation testing

It's amazing how I saw how users adjust to, which for me, is a small change. This proves that not every redesign could be successful.

I consider this a success because I was not aiming to improve the time on task but convenience. Efficiency just came as a result of providing what the users need in the tasks.
